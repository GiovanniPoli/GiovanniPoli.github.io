About
------

My research lies at the intersection of Bayesian methodology and biomedical applications, with a focus on modeling heterogeneity in complex data. During my Ph.D., I developed Bayesian nonparametric methods for principled borrowing of information across related datasets, addressing key limitations of standard statistical approaches.

My work spans multiple areas, including multiple testing in high-dimensional biological data, meta-analysis of heterogeneous studies, and the modeling of time-series. More broadly, my research aims to design flexible yet interpretable statistical models, grounded in Bayesian inference, to support biomedical research.

A central aspect of my research is bridging the gap between advanced statistical methodology and its practical use in clinical research. I am particularly interested in developing methods that remain both theoretically rigorous and accessible to non-specialist users, with the goal of improving reproducibility and interpretability in applied settings.

Research Interests
------
- Bayesian nonparametrics  
- Statistical modeling of heterogeneity  
- Clinical data analysis and biostatistics  
- Reproducibility and interpretability in statistics  
- Applied Bayesian methods for biomedical research  

Current Work
------

My current research focuses on developing novel Bayesian frameworks that integrate flexible modeling with practical usability. This includes designing methodologies that can work alongside standard statistical procedures while providing a principled quantification of uncertainty.

The work is motivated by real-world clinical applications, developed in collaboration with clinical researchers, with a particular focus on oncology and neuroscience.
